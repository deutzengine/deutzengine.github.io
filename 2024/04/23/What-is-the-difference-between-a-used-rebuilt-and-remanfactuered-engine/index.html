What is the difference between a used,rebuilt and remanfactuered engine?

USED: Used diesel engines are pulled directly from a vehicle and generally not even so much as surface cleaned. Used components are not disassembled, internally cleaned or inspected. Junkyard or used components can have high mileage along with a poor maintenance history, and that can be a failure waiting to happen. Many used or junkyard components come from a vehicle that was involved in an accident, so there may be unseen damage.

REBUILT: To rebuild is reconditioning by cleaning, inspecting and replacing severely worn or broken parts. In the rebuild process, serviceable parts are reused within the manufacturer’s acceptable wear limits. Rebuilt component quality can vary widely and many rebuilds come with only a short term warranty.

REMANUFACTURED: Remanufacturing is to make as closely to new as possible. Core material – heads and block castings, crankshaft, camshaft and rods – are carefully inspected and checked against original equipment measured specifications for correct dimensional tolerances then precisely machined. Replacement parts are new or requalified to meet exacting standards and tolerances. Testing is performed to meet the measured specifications of the manufacturer and original production standards.
